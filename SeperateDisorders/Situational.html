Situational Depression

    Overview

    When a stressful event triggers a period of depression, it’s called situational depression. It’s common and you may have experienced it at some point in your life. Situational depression is different from clinical depression. Here are the differences, and what you should know about each type of depression.

    Symptoms

  • Anger
  • Appetite changes
  • Constant fear or worry
  • Difficulty of caring out tasks
  • Fequent Crying
  • Grief

    • Treatments / Tips

  • Call 911 or a Hotline(text or call 988) if you are having Suicidial thoughts
  • Routine Doctor Visits
  • Health Habits
  • Support Group
  • Therapy
  • Prescribed Medication
  • Communicate with People you trust
